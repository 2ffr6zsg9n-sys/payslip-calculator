Welcome to the Paylip Calculator

This calculator can help you estimate your monthly net pay before your actual payslip is produced.

You will be asked one question at a time. You can go back if you need to change an answer, then add any extra allowances or deductions before calculating your estimate.

This calculator gives an estimate only. Your actual payslip is the confirmed record of your pay, deductions and net pay.

How would you like us to estimate your tax?

The estimate is usually more accurate if you enter the taxable pay and tax paid figures from your previous month’s payslip. Choose Week 1/Month 1 if you do not want to enter those figures.

Which payslip do you want to estimate?

We use this to work out which previous payslip figures you should enter. If you choose April, the estimate will be treated like a Week 1/Month 1 calculation.

What is your tax code?

You can find this on your payslip. If you are not sure, leave the default and treat the result as a rough estimate.

Are you a new starter?

What are your year-to-date pay and tax figures?

Use the year-to-date figures from the bottom left-hand side of your latest payslip. Do not enter the monthly taxable pay or monthly tax paid figure.

What is your NI category letter?

This is usually letter A unless your payslip shows something different.

Do you have a student loan?

Which student loan plan is shown on your last payslip?

Do you have a postgraduate loan?

Which postgraduate loan scheme is on your payslip?

Should we use Agenda for Change pay rates?

What monthly salary should we use?

Enter the monthly salary before deductions. If you leave this blank, the calculator will use £0.00 basic pay.

What are your contracted hours?

Full-time Agenda for Change hours are assumed to be 37.5.

What is your Agenda for Change pay band?

Are you in the NHS Pension Scheme?

What pension percentage is shown on your latest payslip?

Choose the band that matches the pension percentage rate shown on your latest payslip. The calculator will suggest one, but you can change it.
